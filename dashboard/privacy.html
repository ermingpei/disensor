Privacy Policy

Last Updated: January 10, 2026

At DiSensor Network, we value your privacy. We are committed to protecting the personal and sensor data you choose to share with us. This policy explains what we collect and how it is used.

1. Data We Collect

DiSensor is a crowd-sourced sensing network. To function, we collect the following types of data from your device:

  • Sensor Data: Barometric pressure, ambient noise levels (dB), magnetic field, and other environmental metrics.
  • Location Data: GPS coordinates are used to map environmental data to specific geographic hexes (H3 indices).
  • Network Signals: WiFi and Bluetooth scan results (SSID, BSSID, RSSI) to analyze signal density.
  • Device ID: A unique, anonymous identifier generated for your device to track contributions and rewards.

2. How We Use Your Data

  • Scientific Research: Aggregated data helps researchers study micro-climates, urban noise pollution, and signal propagation.
  • Network Optimization: Building a global, high-precision map of environmental and network conditions.
  • Rewards Distribution: We use your contribution logs to calculate and distribute Sense Points accurately.

3. Data Protection

All data is transmitted securely using encryption. We do not collect personal identifiers like your name, phone number, or contacts unless you explicitly provide them (e.g., for reward redemption).

Data Retention: We retain your data for up to 2 years for research purposes. After this period, data is anonymized or deleted.

Cross-Border Transfer: Your data may be stored on servers located in North America (Canada/USA). By using DiSensor, you consent to this transfer.

4. Your Rights

You can stop data collection at any time by pausing the "Sensing" feature in the app or revoking permissions in your system settings. You may also request the deletion of your device's data by contacting support.

Under applicable privacy laws (GDPR, PIPEDA), you have the right to: access your data, request correction, request deletion, and withdraw consent.
